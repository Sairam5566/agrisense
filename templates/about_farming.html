About Farming

A beginner‑friendly guide to crops, seasons, soil, irrigation, fertilizers, pest management and best practices.

Crop Seasons in India

India has three major agricultural seasons. Understanding these helps in choosing the right crop for the right time and region.

  • Kharif (June–October) – Sown with monsoon onset; e.g., Rice, Maize, Cotton, Soybean, Groundnut.
  • Rabi (October–March) – Sown after monsoon; e.g., Wheat, Mustard, Gram, Barley, Pea.
  • Zaid (March–June) – Short summer season; e.g., Watermelon, Cucumber, Fodder crops.

Soil Health Basics

  • Test soil every 2–3 years to know pH, organic carbon, N‑P‑K status.
  • Add organic matter (FYM/compost/green manure) to improve structure and water‑holding capacity.
  • Balance nutrients: apply fertilizers as per recommendation and split doses to avoid losses.
  • Maintain residue cover and reduce tillage to prevent erosion.

Irrigation & Water Management

  • Prefer micro‑irrigation (drip/sprinkler) for water savings and higher yields.
  • Irrigate in the early morning or evening to reduce evaporation.
  • Mulch to conserve soil moisture and suppress weeds.
  • Schedule irrigation based on soil moisture and crop stage, not the calendar.

Fertilizers & Nutrient Management

  • Follow the 4R principle: Right source, Right dose, Right time, Right method.
  • Combine chemical fertilizers with organics and bio‑fertilizers (Azotobacter, PSB).
  • Use seed treatment and micronutrients (Zn, B) where deficient.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

  • Choose resistant varieties and healthy certified seeds.
  • Monitor fields regularly; use traps/yellow sticky cards for early detection.
  • Prefer biological controls (Trichogramma, Neem‑based products); use chemicals only when thresholds are crossed.
  • Rotate pesticides with different modes of action to avoid resistance.

Climate‑Smart & Sustainable Practices

  • Diversify with pulses, oilseeds, and horticulture for income stability.
  • Adopt conservation agriculture, intercropping, and agro‑forestry where suitable.
  • Harvest rainwater; use farm ponds and recharge pits.
  • Digitize records for costs, yields, and input use to make better decisions.
